Classical Music

Music is a very interesting topic, it has many interesting styles and genres. This website will provide you EVERYTHING you to need to know about Classical Music. This includes, orchestras, instruments and composers.



Instruments used in the Classical Era

The Piano

The Piano is a percussion instrument. Still used today in Modern Electronic Music,
it is one of the most famous instruments in the music industries.
The Piano was invented by Bartolomeo Cristofori
near the year 1700 in modern day Italy.
Some famous pianists are Frederic Chopin,
Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart and Ludwig van Beethoven.



The Violin

The Violin is a String Instrument, meaning you have to draw a bow to play it.
One of the most popular and learnt instruments in the world, the Violin
is invented by Andrea Amati, an Italian String Instrument Maker.
Amati invented the Violin near the year 1550. Some famous violinists
are Antonio Vivaldi, Niccolo Paganini and
Johann Sebastian Bach.




The Cello

The Cello is a String Instrument, meaning you have to draw a bow or to pluck it to play it.
The Cello is the second largest instrument of the String Family
behind the Double Bass being 180cm tall. The Violin is invented by
Andrea Amati, an Italian String Instrument Maker. Amati invented the first cello in near the
mid 16th Century. The most famous cellists are Johann Sebastian Bach,
Luigi Boccherini and Yo-yo Ma.



The Clarinet

The Clarinet is a woodwind instrument, meaning you have to blow it to create sound.
The Clarinet is one of the most popular woodwind instruments.
The Clarinet was invented by Johann Christoph Denner, a German woodwind
instrument maker. Denner invented the clarinet sometime after 1698. Some of the famous
clarinet composers are Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, Antonio Vivaldi and Benny Goodman.





Composers

Mozart

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart is an Austrian Musician (1756-1791) who played the Piano, Violin and Clarinet.
Mozart was born in Salzburg, Austria and his father, Leopard Mozart was a German violinist.
Mozart is famous for composing many different operas such as The Magic Clarinet and
the Marriage of Figaro and created many symphonies.
The most popular Mozart Compositions are Turkish March, Eine Kleine Nachtmusik and Lacrimosa.
Mozart died in 1791, his death is unknown but is believed that he was poisoned by a rival, Salieri.

Beethoven

Ludwig van Beethoven is a German pianist (1770-1827). Beethoven was born in
Bonn, Germany to Johann van Beethoven. Beethoven is famous for composing many piano
pieces such as Moonlight Sonata No. 14, Fur Elise and Ode to Joy. Beethoven has also
been taught piano by Joseph Haydn, another German Pianist and Composer. Beethoven had also
suffered a hearing problem and at one point, went deaf. Beethoven has also composed many symphonies such as
Symphony No. 5 and Symphony No. 9. Beethoven died in his apartment in 1827 at the age of 57, the reason is
his liver was damaged.

Chopin

Frederic Chopin is a Polish Pianist (1810-1849). Chopin was borin in Poland and went to Vienna, Austria in
November 1830. After 8 months, he headed straight to Paris for the most of the rest of his life
Chopin is famous for composing pieces such as Nocturne No. 20 in C# Minor, Fantasie Impromptu
and Nocturne No. 2. Chopin actually did not write any symphonies. Chopin died in 1849 in his apartment
in Paris, the reason of his death in unknown.
